Exam 1 — Modeling Problems

Due Friday, May 8 at 11:59 PM

Exam 1 has two parts: the modeling workbook below (emailed to me) and a multiple-choice component posted on Blackboard. Both are due by 11:59 PM on Friday, May 8.

Three modeling problems in the workbook. Each problem has its own instructions tab with step-by-step guidance and the questions you need to answer.

  1. Bollinger Bands — build a 20-day moving average with ±1σ and ±2σ bands on Tesla daily prices, then chart it. Empirical Rule applied to a rolling window.
  2. Sector Allocation — work through the S&P 500 list and decide how to allocate 100 portfolio positions across the 11 GICS sectors. Compare strategies on cost, time, and rigor.
  3. Pro Forma — build a simplified Procter & Gamble income statement for FY26 using the historical data and the scenario probabilities. Use the Empirical Rule to bound the COGS estimate.

Rules

  • Open everything. You can use any tool, any AI, any source. The goal is to produce a working model.
  • Show your work in the spreadsheet. Use formulas and cell references — not pasted values. I'm grading the model, not just the answers.
  • Submit by email. Save your filled-in workbook and email it to russell.holloway@austincc.edu before the deadline. Subject line: 15680 — Exam 1 submission.
  • If anything looks broken — an instruction that doesn't match the model, a cell that won't calculate, an unclear question — email me or post in the discussion. Don't guess.
