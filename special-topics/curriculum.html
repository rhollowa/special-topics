16-Week Semester Arc

Curriculum

From company selection to a measurable launch outcome — every week ties back to one of the four course objectives.

Status

Working draft

The curriculum below is an outline scaffolded against the four course objectives. Detailed week-by-week content will be populated as the course is finalized for the Fall 2026 soft launch.

Phase 01

Foundation & Industry Selection

Weeks 1–3 · Maps to Objective 1 (Analyze)

Week 1 — Course foundation & AI tooling. Course orientation, AI tooling setup (Google Gemini through ACC Workspace), introduction to AI agents as a managed workforce. Students get their first agent and learn the difference between using AI as a tool vs. managing AI as a worker.

Week 2 — Industry selection & opportunity scan. Students choose a GICS sector and a specific company concept. Initial competitive landscape sweep using AI-directed research. Distinguishing real signal from AI hallucination.

Week 3 — Competitive analysis deliverable. Students produce an industry analysis using AI agents to gather, structure, and verify market intelligence. Deliverable: Competitive landscape report (Objective 1).

Phase 02

Market Research & Validation

Weeks 4–6 · Maps to Objective 2 (Create & Evaluate)

Week 4 — Primary research design. Survey design, sampling strategy, instrument validation. AI agents as a force multiplier for primary research — what they're good for, where they fall short.

Week 5 — Data collection & synthesis. Running the research study with AI-supported data collection. Cleaning AI-mediated data and protecting against AI-generated bias.

Week 6 — Go/No-Go recommendation. Synthesizing research into a launch recommendation with quantified confidence. Deliverable: Market research study with go/no-go recommendation (Objective 2).

Phase 03

Business Plan & AI Workforce Build

Weeks 7–11 · Maps to Objective 3 (Construct)

Week 7 — Entity formation & organizational design. Choosing a business entity (C-Corp, LLC, sole proprietorship). Designing an org chart that includes AI roles alongside human roles.

Week 8 — Financial planning. Pro-forma financial statements, capital strategy, pricing model. AI-assisted modeling and what gets verified vs. trusted.

Week 9 — Go-to-market strategy. Customer segmentation, channel strategy, customer acquisition path. AI-supported marketing operations and the human override checkpoints.

Week 10 — AI workforce build. Defining roles for AI agents, writing operational specs, designing quality-control loops. Documentation that another manager could read and execute.

Week 11 — Business plan delivery. Integrated business plan with GTM strategy and AI workforce documentation. Deliverable: Complete business plan (Objective 3).

Phase 04

Launch & Performance Evaluation

Weeks 12–16 · Maps to Objective 4 (Evaluate & Justify)

Week 12 — Launch operations. Activating the AI workforce. Running the business with the documented quality-control loops in place.

Week 13 — Performance monitoring. Reading the dashboards. Identifying where AI output is on-target vs. drifting. First override decisions.

Week 14 — Adjustment & iteration. Course-correcting in real time. Documenting what changed, why, and what the human judgment call was.

Week 15 — Final performance review. Measuring against the benchmarks set in Phase 03. Honest assessment of what worked, what didn't, and what required a human in the loop.

Week 16 — Post-mortem & presentation. Final presentation with override log and lessons learned. Deliverable: Performance review with documented override decisions (Objective 4).

Threading the semester

What runs every week

Weekly business news loop. Each class opens with a current Wall Street Journal article and a discussion of how the news ripples through each student's industry and company.

Weekly AI tooling check-in. A standing review of new AI capabilities, tools, and limitations released that week — keeping the course content honest as the landscape evolves.

Weekly override journal. Students log every decision where they overrode or redirected AI output, building the artifact that will be evaluated in Objective 4.
