Crisis support

If you're in immediate danger, call 911. For thoughts of harming yourself or others, please call a crisis hotline now — they're staffed 24/7 and the call is free and confidential.

If you are struggling with a mental health or personal crisis, any of the numbers below connect you with trained counselors. You don't need to be in immediate danger to call — these lines are there for anyone who needs to talk.

  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline 988 or 1-800-273-TALK (8255)
  • Crisis Text Line Text "home" to 741741
  • Austin / Travis County 24-hour Crisis & Suicide 512-472-HELP (4357)
  • Williamson County 24-hour Crisis 1-800-841-1255
  • Bastrop County Family Crisis Center 1-888-311-7755
  • Hays County 24-hour Crisis 1-877-466-0660
  • SAMHSA National Helpline (substance & mental health) 1-800-662-HELP (4357)
  • NAMI Helpline (mental illness support) 1-800-950-NAMI (6264)

A note from your instructor

The resources on this page aren't just a checklist ACC requires me to include — they're a real list of things that help real students finish the course. I've had students use every single one of them. If something on this page might help you, please use it. If you're not sure what you need, email me and we'll figure it out together.

And if you need to skip class to take care of something in your life, tell me. I'd rather hear "I'm dealing with X this week" than have a student disappear.
