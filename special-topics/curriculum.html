16-Week Semester Arc

Curriculum

From company selection to a measurable launch outcome — every week ties back to one of the four course objectives.

Phase 01

Foundation & Industry Selection

Weeks 1–3 · Maps to Objective 1 (Analyze)

Week 1 — Course foundation & AI tooling. Course orientation, AI tooling setup (Google Gemini through ACC Workspace), introduction to AI agents as a managed workforce. Students get their first agent and learn the difference between using AI as a tool vs. managing AI as a worker.

Week 2 — Industry selection & opportunity scan. Students choose a GICS sector and a specific company concept — partitioning the class so every industry gets a leader by end of semester. Initial competitive landscape sweep using AI-directed research. Distinguishing real signal from AI hallucination.

Week 3 — Competitive analysis deliverable. Students produce an industry analysis using AI agents to gather, structure, and verify market intelligence. Entity-type decision finalized — C-Corp, LLC, or Sole Proprietorship — a fork that shapes the entire semester (board governance vs. capital-raising path vs. tortoise-mode autonomy). Deliverable: Competitive landscape report (Objective 1).

Phase 02

Market Research & Validation

Weeks 4–6 · Maps to Objective 2 (Create & Evaluate)

Week 4 — Primary research design. Survey design, sampling strategy, instrument validation. AI agents as a force multiplier for primary research — what they're good for, where they fall short.

Week 5 — Data collection & synthesis. Running the research study with AI-supported data collection. Cleaning AI-mediated data and protecting against AI-generated bias.

Week 6 — Go/No-Go recommendation. Synthesizing research into a launch recommendation with quantified confidence. Deliverable: Market research study with go/no-go recommendation (Objective 2).

Phase 03

Business Plan & AI Workforce Build

Weeks 7–11 · Maps to Objective 3 (Construct)

Week 7 — Entity formation & organizational design. Operationalizing the entity choice from Phase 01. C-Corp students draft executive compensation and golden-parachute clauses; LLC students file operating agreements; sole props document personal liability exposure. Designing an org chart that includes AI roles alongside human roles.

Week 8 — Financial planning. Pro-forma financial statements, capital strategy, pricing model. AI-assisted modeling and what gets verified vs. trusted. LLC milestone: a VC/PE-style pitch round to raise growth capital — a live lesson in dilution and what strings come with outside money.

Week 9 — Go-to-market strategy. Customer segmentation, channel strategy, customer acquisition path. Each student gets a bespoke TourcasT referral link; the geographic spread of their network shows up live on the platform's globe and becomes a strategic asset (or liability) heading into Phase 04.

Week 10 — AI workforce build. Defining roles for AI agents, writing operational specs, designing quality-control loops. Documentation that another manager could read and execute.

Week 11 — Business plan delivery. Integrated business plan with GTM strategy and AI workforce documentation. Deliverable: Complete business plan (Objective 3).

Two-section twist (when enrollment supports it): If the course runs two sections, one becomes the US economic zone and one becomes the EU economic zone — same curriculum, different regulatory environment (GDPR vs. US privacy law, different trade dynamics, different crisis exposure). Same lectures, international-business curriculum without adding a single class meeting.

Phase 04

Launch & Performance Evaluation

Weeks 12–16 · Maps to Objective 4 (Evaluate & Justify)

Week 12 — Launch operations. Activating the AI workforce. Running the business with the documented quality-control loops in place. First real revenue lands — referral revenue from Phase 03 flows into the live P&L.

Week 13 — Performance monitoring. Reading the dashboards. Identifying where AI output is on-target vs. drifting. First override decisions logged.

Week 14 — The War Game (24-hour stress test). A weekend simulation that stress-tests every decision made all semester. Students receive cascading inbox events — macroeconomic shocks, regulatory changes, demand swings, supply-chain disruptions, even competitor actions from classmates in the same GICS sector. The geographic spread of their referral network is now a live variable: concentrated networks are exposed, diversified networks are resilient. C-Corp students can be voted out by their board if the company tanks (they walk with whatever golden parachute they negotiated in Week 7 — or with nothing); LLC students can elect to IPO mid-game, a live lesson in dilution and SEC disclosure. The students who built clean delegation can sleep through the night; the micromanagers pull an all-nighter.

Week 15 — Davos: The Economic Summit. The capstone. One industry leader per GICS sector presents the state of their industry to the room — pulling up the TourcasT globe to show their sector's combined geographic footprint, walking through revenue (simulated war-game revenue plus real referral revenue), and analyzing every company in their sector across both economic zones. The map tells the story before anyone opens their mouth. Industry leaders are graded by the instructor, by self-assessment, and by every participant in their sector — the same accountability shape an executive presenting to a board lives with in the real world.

Week 16 — Post-mortem & final review. Performance review against Phase 03 benchmarks. Honest assessment of what worked, what didn't, and which decisions required a human in the loop. Deliverable: Performance review with documented override decisions (Objective 4).

Threading the semester

What runs every week

Weekly business news loop. Each class opens with a current Wall Street Journal article and a discussion of how the news ripples through each student's industry and company.

Weekly AI tooling check-in. A standing review of new AI capabilities, tools, and limitations released that week — keeping the course content honest as the landscape evolves.

Weekly override journal. Students log every decision where they overrode or redirected AI output, building the artifact that will be evaluated in Objective 4.
